# Rinkari clue style guide

Read this before drafting a single clue. These standards came from studying
thousands of published clues; they are what separates a Rinkari clue from
machine output.

## The Link — two valid forms
Most days the Link is a **compound** word (every answer + LINK makes a real
word). Some days it's a **category** (every answer is simply a member —
BIRDS, COLOURS, CHESS PIECES). Category days need no compounding logic in the
clues at all — just clue the five answers fairly and let the shared category
be the discovery. Don't hint the category in any single clue's surface.

## The anatomy
Every clue = DEFINITION + WORDPLAY, nothing else. The definition sits at one
edge. The wordplay independently constructs the same answer. A solver must be
able to reach the answer from either half and confirm with the other.

## Surface first
The clue must read as a natural sentence about something — a tiny story, an
image, a complaint, a headline. "Some fussy diner might send back desserts,
feeling anxious (8)" works because you picture the diner. If the surface only
exists to smuggle wordplay, rewrite it. Test: read it aloud to an imaginary
stranger; if they'd hear a sentence, it passes.

## Misdirection through part of speech
The best clues make a word look like one part of speech while it functions as
another. "Flag" reads as noun, works as verb. "Returned" reads as narrative,
works as reversal indicator. Aim for at least one such pivot per clue.

## Device standards
- **Hidden**: answer spans word boundaries where possible (pleaSUREs is fine,
  spanning two words is better). Indicator must genuinely signal containment:
  some, partly, amount of, held by, in.
- **Anagram**: fodder must appear verbatim in the surface; indicator must
  signal disorder (cooked, treated, broken, confused, novel, at sea). Never
  use an indirect anagram (synonym-then-scramble).
- **Reversal**: indicator signals direction (returned, back, over, brought up
  in a down clue — we have no down clues, so use returned/back/reflected).
- **Charade**: parts in order, each part fairly clued (D = daughter, C = about/
  circa, O = love). Standard abbreviations only.
- **Container**: indicator signals insertion/surrounding (about, holding,
  wearing, without = outside).
- **Double definition**: both definitions must be genuinely distinct senses,
  ideally different parts of speech. Two-word DDs ("Essential island") are
  elegant; long DDs get flabby.

## Fairness lines we never cross
- No definition-by-example without "say"/"perhaps".
- No obscure answers: every answer is a word a normal reader knows.
- Enumeration always given and always correct.
- The Link must never be guessable from any single clue's surface.

## Tone
Wit over difficulty. A solver should finish feeling clever, not bludgeoned.
When in doubt, make it kinder and funnier.
