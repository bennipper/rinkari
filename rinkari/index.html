How to play

Solve the five cryptic clues, then find the Link — usually one word that attaches to every answer to make a new word or phrase (CAMP, SURE, SPIT → all take ‘FIRE’). Some days the Link is a shared category instead — five birds, five colours, five card games — and you're guessing what connects them. Clues are worth 3 points each; every hint used costs 1, down to a floor of 0 — so if you get truly stuck, keep tapping Hint and it will reveal one more letter each time until the whole word is filled in. The Link is worth 5, wrong guesses cost 1. The first two hints teach as they help: the first shows the definition, the second names the wordplay.

Every cryptic clue has two halves

A definition (always at the start or end) and wordplay that builds the same answer a second way. The surface meaning is a decoy — read it like a machine, not a sentence.

The wordplay devices

Anagram — letters rearranged, flagged by words like broken, cooked, wild.
Tablet smashed in conflict (6) → BATTLE
Hidden word — the answer sits inside the clue, flagged by some, partly, amount of.
Some sublime fruit (4) → LIME
Charade — the answer built from parts, front to back.
Fine whip, very showy (5) → F + LASH
Double definition — two meanings, one word, no wordplay at all.
Angry hybrid (5) → CROSS
Container — one word placed inside another, flagged by about, holding, trapped in.
Abrupt about love (5) → C(O)URT
Reversal — a word read backwards, flagged by returned, sent back, turned.
Skewer gratuities returned (4) → SPIT
